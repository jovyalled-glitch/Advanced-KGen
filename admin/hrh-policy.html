Purpose and Scope This policy sets out the principles, standards, and processes governing the management of employees across KGen Trust (Test). It ensures that employment practices are fair, legally compliant, and consistent with the Department for Education (DfE) Staffing and Employment Guidance for Schools (2025), and align with the Trustâ€™s mission to provide a high-quality education through effective, empowered, and motivated people. The policy applies to all Trust employees, including permanent, temporary, and fixed-term staff. It also applies to supply, agency, and contractor personnel where appropriate. 2. Legal and Regulatory Framework KGen Trust (Test) complies with relevant employment and education legislation, including: - Employment Rights Act 1996 - Equality Act 2010 - Maternity and Parental Leave Regulations 1999 - Data Protection Act 2018 / UK GDPR - Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 - Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) 2025 - DfE Staffing and Employment Guidance for Schools (September 2025) - Academy Trust Handbook (ESFA 2025) The Trust also meets obligations under the Public Sector Equality Duty (s.149 Equality Act 2010). 3. Policy Objectives The HR Policy aims to: - Support the delivery of the HR Service Catalogue by providing a legal and procedural framework for implementation. - Ensure compliance with statutory employment legislation and DfE expectations. - Promote fairness, equity, and transparency in employment decisions. - Provide clear expectations and standards for HR service delivery across the Trust. - Enable effective people management through good governance, accountability, and communication. 4. HR Service Domains and Standards Aligned with the HR Service Catalogue, KGen Trust (Test) operates across eight key domains: **5.1 Recruitment & Onboarding** - Safer Recruitment in line with KCSIE 2025. - Transparent advertising and fair selection. - Full induction and safeguarding training within 10 days of joining. - Probation reviews at 3 and 6 months. **5.2 Employment Lifecycle** - Written contracts and prompt variation management. - Absence, performance, and ER cases managed per Trust policy. - Formal exit and leaver process including exit interviews. **5.3 Payroll, Reward & Benefits** - Payroll accuracy assured through collaboration with Finance. - Pay Policy aligned to STPCD and NJC terms. - Pensions via TPS and LGPS schemes. - EAP and wellbeing initiatives available Trust-wide. **5.4 Family Leave (Maternity, Paternity, Adoption, SPL)** - 52 weeksâ€™ maternity leave (26 ordinary + 26 additional). - Statutory and Occupational Maternity Pay applied per eligibility. - Notification required by the 15th week before expected childbirth. - Risk assessments for pregnant employees conducted promptly. - Two weeksâ€™ statutory paternity leave available. - Adoption leave mirrors maternity provisions. - Shared Parental Leave up to 50 weeks (37 weeks paid) available. - Parental Bereavement Leave available (2 weeks). **5.5 Compliance & Safeguarding** - SCR maintained and updated daily. - Compliance with Equality, H&S, and Data Protection laws. **5.6 HR Systems & Data** - HR records managed securely in line with GDPR. - Monthly data reporting and workforce analytics. **5.7 Learning, Development & Performance** - Annual appraisals aligned to Trust priorities. - CPD Catalogue for professional development. - Succession planning supported across schools. **5.8 Strategic HR & Organisational Design** - HR supports workforce planning, restructures, and change. - Consultation per ACAS and DfE guidance with equality impact analysis. 6. Monitoring and Performance HR performance monitored through monthly KPIs (turnover, absence, recruitment) and quarterly reporting to the Trust Board. Annual HR Service Review ensures compliance with the Service Catalogue and DfE standards. 7. Policy Review and Updates This policy is reviewed annually by the Director of People and approved by the Trust Board. It may be reviewed earlier following legislative or organisational changes. 8.
